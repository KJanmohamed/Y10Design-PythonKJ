    • ~1800

    Thomas Chandler Haliburton The Birth

    A man who is still North America’s most quoted author, Thomas Chandler Haliburton, born in Windsor in 1796, told of King’s College School boys playing “hurley on the ice” when he was a young student at the school around 1800. This is the earliest reference in English literature of a stick-ball game being played on ice in Canada. Below is a stick that was auctioned that is believed to be used in the 1800's.

    1950-1967

    The NHL The Original Six Years

    This was when the NHL really started taking a turn towards organized hockey. Stability was a good thing in Montreal, Detroit and Toronto. These three teams missed the playoffs just 5 times over this period and, starting in 1942, won 25 of 26 Stanley Cups. Stability was more like a jail sentence in Chicago, Boston and New York, where a top two finish was awfully rare (six times). These were the six teams who "started" the game of hockey. Moreover, this was the years where the mask was invented.

    1987-2007

    NHL Expansion Equipment Era

    Expansion resumed in the 1990s with the NHL growing from 21 to 30 teams but the scoring trend broke. For the first time in the history of the NHL, expansion was associated with a contraction in the average number of goals per game. This included many changes to equipment, including skates, sticks and the goaltenders equipment. The NHL was in full force.

    2008-2019

    Modern NHL Hockey as we see today

    This is how we see hockey today. The rules were finalized throughout this time period along with the changes in equipment to allow for more mobility and range. This comes with the emergence of hockey players such as Connor McDavid and Auston Matthews, two of the best hockey players in todays time period.
